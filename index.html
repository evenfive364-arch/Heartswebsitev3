Who We Are

Healing where body,
spirit, and land meet.

We are dedicated to supporting rangatahi Māori, survivors of sexual abuse, and those navigating suicidal ideation through culturally grounded, holistic care. Rooted in tikanga Māori, our work addresses intergenerational trauma by fostering kotahitanga (unity), strengthening whanaungatanga (connection), and honouring whakapapa (genealogy).

Through equine-supported therapy, systemic family constellation work, and collaboration with clinical professionals, we take a whole-person approach — mind, body, and spirit. Guided by the principles of waiora, we create safe, inclusive spaces for healing, empowering individuals to reconnect with themselves, their communities, and the possibility of a more life-affirming future.

Our Practice

The medicine circle —
where the unseen
becomes visible.

We work alongside ngā hōiho within a medicine circle — a sacred relational space where what is unseen can be safely revealed. Each horse holds a unique therapeutic presence, representing aspects of connection, truth, strength, vulnerability, and love.

Without words needing to be spoken, the horses respond to the emotional and relational field of the person present. Where they stand within the circle reflects what is happening beneath the surface — the places of longing, disconnection, or unresolved experience.

Through this process, we are able to gently and directly access the core relational wounds that may sit within a person — often shaped by lived experience, and at times carried across generations.

  • Nervous system regulation — feeling safe in the body
  • Relational repair — experiencing trust, attunement, and connection
  • Ancestral awareness — recognising patterns carried through whakapapa
  • Return to self — reconnecting with inherent strength and light

"Healing is not something done to a person — it is something that emerges through relationship. Through kotahitanga with ngā hōiho, the path of healing, self-discovery, and transformation is revealed."

Our Values

Guided by what endures.

01

Kotahitanga

Unity & Collective Connection

Healing through shared belonging. We hold space for individuals and communities to reconnect — with themselves, with each other, and with the whenua.

02

Whanaungatanga

Relationships & Belonging

Our work centres the relationships that make us whole — human, horse, and the living relational systems that shape us across generations.
